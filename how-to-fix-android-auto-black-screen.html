# How To Fix Android Auto Black Screen

Encountering a black screen when trying to use Android Auto is a common and frustrating problem. This comprehensive guide provides technical troubleshooting steps to diagnose and resolve the issue, helping you get back to seamless navigation and entertainment.

1. Essential First Steps

* **Restart Everything:** Begin by restarting your Android phone. Then, turn off your car, open and close the driver's door (to ensure the infotainment system powers down), wait a minute, and restart the car.
* **Reconnect the USB Cable:** Unplug the USB cable from both your phone and the car's port, then reconnect firmly. Ensure it's fully seated at both ends.

2. Inspect Your USB Cable and Ports

This is frequently the root cause of connectivity issues.
* **Use a Quality Data Cable:** Only use high-quality, short (under 3 feet) USB cables specifically designed for data transfer. The original cable that came with your phone is often the best choice. Many charging-only cables will not work.
* **Check for Damage:** Carefully inspect the cable for any visible damage, kinks, or bent connectors.
* **Try a Different Cable:** Always test with a known good, high-quality data cable.
* **Clean Ports:** Gently clean your phone's and car's USB ports. Use a non-metallic tool (like a wooden toothpick) or compressed air to remove lint, dust, or debris.

3. Clear Android Auto App Data and Cache

Corrupted app data or cache can prevent proper operation.
* **On Your Phone:**
1. Navigate to `Settings` > `Apps` (or `Apps & notifications`) > `See all apps`.
2. Find and tap `Android Auto`.
3. Tap `Storage & cache`.
4. First, tap `Clear cache`. Test Android Auto.
5. If the problem persists, return and tap `Clear storage` (or `Clear data`). **Warning:** This will reset Android Auto settings, requiring re-setup.
* **Repeat for Google Play Services:** Perform the same `Clear cache` then `Clear storage` steps for `Google Play Services`, which is critical for Android Auto's functionality.

4. Update and Reinstall Software

Ensure all relevant software is up-to-date.
* **Update Android Auto App:** Open the `Google Play Store`, search for `Android Auto`, and tap `Update` if available.
* **Update Phone OS:** Check for system updates via `Settings` > `System` > `System update`.
* **Reinstall Android Auto:** If updates don't fix it, uninstall the Android Auto app from your phone, then reinstall it from the Google Play Store.

5. Verify Permissions and USB Configuration

* **App Permissions:** Go to `Settings` > `Apps` > `Android Auto` > `Permissions`. Ensure all necessary permissions (e.g., `Files and media`, `Location`, `Phone`) are granted.
* **Default USB Configuration:** If you have Developer Options enabled, go to `Settings` > `System` > `Developer options`. Locate `Default USB configuration` and ensure it's set to `File transfer / Android Auto` or `MTP (Media Transfer Protocol)`.

6. Car Head Unit Specific Checks

* **Check Car's Software:** Consult your vehicle's manual or dealership for instructions on checking and updating your car's infotainment system software.
* **Factory Reset Head Unit:** As a last resort, some car systems allow a factory reset of the head unit. This will erase all personalized settings. Refer to your car's manual before proceeding.

**Conclusion:**
A black screen on Android Auto is typically a solvable issue, often due to a faulty cable or software glitch. By diligently following these technical steps, you should be able to restore functionality. If the problem persists, contacting your phone manufacturer or car dealership for model-specific support may be necessary.
